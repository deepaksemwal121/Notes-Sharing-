Motion

An object is said to be in motion when it changes its position relative to a reference point.
Rest and motion are relative in nature.

Types of motion
R
Random motion
Irregular path, no fixed direction. E.g. motion of gas molecules, flying insects.
T
Translational
Body moves from one point to another in a straight line. E.g. car on highway.
O
Oscillatory
Body moves to and fro about a fixed point. E.g. pendulum of a clock.
C
Circular
Body moves along a circular path. E.g. Earth around Sun, fan blades.
Scalar vs vector quantities

Scalar quantity

Has magnitude and unit only — no direction.
Examples: Distance, Speed, Work, Time, Mass

Vector quantity

Has magnitude, unit and direction.
Examples: Displacement, Velocity, Acceleration, Force
Distance & displacement
D
Distance
Actual path length travelled between two points.
Scalar — has magnitude only, no direction.
SI unit: metre (m)
Distance is always ≥ displacement. Never negative.
S
Displacement
Shortest straight-line distance joining initial and final positions, with direction.
Vector — has magnitude and direction.
SI unit: metre (m)
Can be zero, positive or negative. E.g. if you walk 100 m and return to same point, displacement = 0 but distance = 200 m.
Speed & velocity
v
Speed
Distance travelled per unit time.
Speed = Distance / Time
Scalar quantity — no direction.
SI unit: m/s
Average speed
Avg Speed = Total distance / Total time
Uniform motion
Equal distance in equal intervals of time. E.g. A —100m— B —100m— C in 20s each.
Non-uniform motion
Unequal distance in equal or unequal intervals. E.g. moving in a busy market.
v
Velocity
Displacement per unit time.
Velocity = Displacement / Time
Vector quantity — has direction.
SI unit: m/s
Average velocity
Avg Velocity = Total displacement / Total time
If body returns to same point, avg velocity = 0 (displacement = 0) but avg speed ≠ 0.
Acceleration
a
Acceleration
Change in velocity per unit time.
a = (v − u) / t
Vector quantity SI unit: m/s²
Uniform acceleration: constant change in velocity. E.g. free fall under gravity.
Symbols used
u initial velocity
v final velocity
s displacement
a uniform acceleration
t time duration
Negative acceleration = retardation (object slowing down).
Equations of motion (for uniform acceleration)
=
Three equations of motion
1st equation
v = u + at
velocity–time relation
2nd equation
s = ut + ½at²
position–time relation
3rd equation
v² = u² + 2as
velocity–position relation
These equations are valid only for uniform (constant) acceleration in a straight line.
Graphical representation
P
Position–Time graph
Slope of P–T graph = Velocity
Body at rest
Uniform motion (constant slope)
Non-uniform motion (varying slope)
V
Velocity–Time graph
Slope of V–T graph = Acceleration
Area under V–T graph = Displacement
Constant velocity (zero acceleration)
Uniform +ve acceleration (straight line up)
Uniform −ve acceleration / retardation
Non-uniform acceleration (curve)
Area under V–T graph = displacement
A
Displacement from V–T graph (trapezium)
Area of trapezium OABC = Displacement
Displacement = Area under V-T graph
For uniform acceleration, the area is a trapezium (or triangle if starts from rest).
Uniform circular motion
O
Uniform circular motion
Body moves in a circular path at constant speed but velocity changes continuously because direction changes at every point.
Since velocity changes (direction), there is acceleration even though speed is constant.
Direction of velocity is always tangential to the circle at that point.
v = 2πR / T
Where R = radius of circle, T = time period for one complete revolution.
Examples
Earth revolving around Sun Moon around Earth Tip of clock hands Fan blades Stone tied to a string whirled in circle
Quick summary — graph slope & area rules
P-T
Position–Time graph
Slope = Velocity
Horizontal line → body at rest
Straight inclined line → uniform motion
Curved line → non-uniform motion
V-T
Velocity–Time graph
Slope = Acceleration
Area under graph = Displacement
Horizontal line → constant velocity (a = 0)
Straight line up → uniform +ve acceleration
Straight line down → retardation (−ve accln)
Curve → non-uniform acceleration
